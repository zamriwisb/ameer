Banana-oat breakfast bowls

Naturally sweet, fibre-rich and ready in ten minutes flat — this is the no-fuss breakfast your little ones will actually finish, and you’ll feel good about serving.

Team Ameer Published 28 March 2026 3 min read

Mornings are hectic, and a breakfast that’s quick and nourishing can feel like a unicorn. Enter the banana-oat bowl: oats give slow-release energy, banana brings natural sweetness (no added sugar needed), and the toppings are completely up to your little chef.

  • ⏱ 10 minutes
  • 🍽 Serves 2 kids
  • 👶 Age 1+
  • 🌙 No added sugar

What you’ll need

  • 1 ripe banana, mashed
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1¼ cups milk (or your preferred milk)
  • A pinch of cinnamon
  • Toppings: berries, a spoon of yoghurt, a sprinkle of seeds

How to make it

1

Simmer

Add oats, milk and the mashed banana to a small pot. Stir over medium heat for 4–5 minutes until creamy.

2

Season

Stir in a pinch of cinnamon. Taste — the banana usually makes it sweet enough, no sugar required.

3

Top & serve

Spoon into bowls and let the kids decorate with berries, yoghurt and seeds. A “rainbow” bowl always wins.

“I make a big batch on Sunday and reheat it through the week. My twins love adding their own toppings — breakfast battles, gone.” — Nadia, Selangor

Make it your own

Swap berries for grated apple and raisins, stir in a spoon of nut butter for extra staying power, or blend it smooth for younger babies. Want to support digestion at the same time? Stir a scoop of flavourless prebiotic fiber straight into the warm oats — it dissolves clear and they’ll never know.

That’s it — one pot, ten minutes, and a breakfast that fuels a busy morning of play.
